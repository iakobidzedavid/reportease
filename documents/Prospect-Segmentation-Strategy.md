## Engagement Scoring Logic

### High Engagement (+3 points each)
- **Recent activity:** Data refreshed < 14 days ago
- **C-level title:** CMO, VP Marketing, VP Growth (has P&L authority)
- **Complete data:** Email + phone + LinkedIn all available
- **Vertical alignment:** Works in B2B SaaS, E-commerce, or Professional Services
- **Company size:** 30-50 employees (mature enough to prioritize tools)

### Medium Engagement (+2 points each)
- **Manager/Director title:** Marketing Manager, Marketing Director, Senior Manager
- **Good data freshness:** 15-30 days old
- **Near-complete data:** 2 of 3 contact channels available
- **Neutral verticals:** Manufacturing, healthcare, retail, financial services
- **Company size:** 10-30 employees

### Low Engagement (-1 point if present)
- **Unverified email:** Not confirmed by Apollo validation
- **Stale data:** > 30 days old
- **Incomplete contact:** Missing phone or LinkedIn
- **Unclear role:** "Contact person" rather than marketing decision-maker

### Scoring Calculation
```
Engagement Score = Σ(High points) + Σ(Medium points) - Σ(Low points)

Score Mapping:
7-9 points = High engagement (Tier 1)
4-6 points = Medium engagement (Tier 2)
1-3 points = Low engagement (Tier 3)
```

## Outreach Sequencing

### Week 1: Tier 1 Warm Outreach
**Day 1-2:** Manual LinkedIn outreach to all 9 Tier 1 prospects (profile view, connection request)  
**Day 3-4:** Phone calls (2-3 min discovery) to those who accept connection  
**Day 5:** Personalized email from CEO w/ relevant competitive threat angle  
**Day 6-7:** Follow-up calls to those who opened email

**Expected Outcomes:**
- 3 qualified demos booked
- 1-2 pilots signed (50% conversion)
- 2-3 warm leads (interest but need stakeholder approval)

### Week 2-3: Tier 2 Cold Email + LinkedIn
**Day 1:** Segment Tier 2 into 4 geographic/vertical clusters  
**Day 2-5:** Cluster-specific cold email #1 (subject: "Marketing agencies in [city] are…")  
**Day 6-7:** LinkedIn follow-up on unopened emails

**Email Variants by Vertical:**
1. **B2B SaaS:** "Weekly lead reports for [Company] clients — automated"
2. **E-commerce:** "ROAS dashboard that clients actually read — built in 2 min"
3. **Professional Services:** "Client retention reports that close more deals"

**Expected Outcomes:**
- 10-15% email open rate (5-6 opens)
- 3-5 qualified leads
- 1-2 demos scheduled

### Week 4: Nurture + Scale
**Day 1-3:** Webinar outreach to all Tier 2 who didn't convert (group validation angle)  
**Day 4-5:** Ad targeting to Tier 3 + others (LinkedIn/Google)  
**Ongoing:** Content nurture (LinkedIn posts, case studies, product updates)

**Expected Outcomes:**
- 2-5 webinar registrations (new demos)
- 1-2 conversions from nurture track
- Momentum for month 2

## Competitive Intelligence

### Pre-Outreach Research Checklist
For each Tier 1 and Tier 2A/2B prospect, collect:
1. **Company website:** Review services, case studies, clientele
2. **LinkedIn company page:** Check recent posts, employee count, news
3. **Decision-maker profile:** Recent activity, connections, interests
4. **Google search:** Recent news, funding, leadership changes
5. **Competitor mapping:** What reporting tools do they currently use? (estimate from their website/careers page)

### Personalization Data Points
- Mention specific clients they work with (if public)
- Reference recent news about company growth/expansion
- Highlight competitor agencies using AutoReport (when available)
- Quantify time savings specific to their agency size (e.g., "As a 28-person shop, you're likely managing 40+ client reporting streams")

## Contingency Plans

### If Tier 1 Conversion < 25% (fewer than 2 pilots)
- Escalate Tier 2A (geographic clusters) to warm outreach
- Request warm introductions via industry networks
- Adjust messaging based on early rejection patterns

### If Tier 2 Conversion < 10% (fewer than 3 pilots)
- Increase ad spend on proven performer segments
- Conduct 3-5 rejection interviews to refine positioning
- Move to account-based marketing on top 5 prospects

### If Total Conversion < 8% (fewer than 4 pilots total)
- Consider beachhead market pivot (move to different company size or vertical)
- Return to personas (Step 5) and messaging (Step 8) validation
