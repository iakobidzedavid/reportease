## Executive Summary

This Assumptions Register documents 15 critical business, product, and market assumptions that underpin ReportEase's go-to-market strategy and business model. Each assumption is categorized, evaluated for impact, and paired with a specific validation experiment. The document serves as a living risk management framework—assumptions will be tested, updated, and refined as data emerges from market experiments.

**Current Risk Level:** HIGH (unvalidated assumptions)
**Business Model Confidence:** 1.4/10 (per GTM assessment)
**Objective:** Move from theoretical to validated assumptions through systematic experimentation

## Part 1: Assumptions Register (15 Critical Assumptions)

### CATEGORY 1: MARKET & CUSTOMER ASSUMPTIONS

#### ASSUMPTION #1: Beachhead Market Selection is Optimal
- **Assumption Statement:** Digital marketing agencies (10-50 person shops) are the ideal beachhead market; they represent the highest willingness to pay, fastest sales cycle, and strongest product-market fit compared to in-house marketing teams or large enterprises.
- **Why It Matters:** If beachhead is wrong, TAM is overstated, sales cycles are longer, and pricing is misaligned. A 6-month pivot to a different segment would delay revenue by 180+ days.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - Industry research suggests agencies spend $1,200-2,000/year per user on reporting solutions (HubSpot marketplace pricing)
  - 100,000 target accounts identified in US (MarketWatch industry reports)
  - $120M TAM calculated
  - Assumption: agencies value time savings more than enterprises
- **Validation Method:** A/B landing page targeting (send traffic from: agency-focused vs. enterprise-focused ads; measure conversion rate ratio). Survey decision-makers at 20 agencies and 20 in-house teams about time-to-decision and budget approval process.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - Agency segment converts at >3x rate vs. enterprise segment (landing page)
  - >70% of surveyed agencies confirm 2-4 week sales cycle
  - >80% report budget authority at VP/Director level (not C-suite)
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - Split Google Ads traffic 50/50 to two landing page variants
  - Variant A: "Built for Marketing Agencies" (headlines, imagery, case studies from agencies)
  - Variant B: "Built for Enterprise Teams" (enterprise language, compliance messaging)
  - Measure: conversion rate (form submission), trial sign-up rate, CAC by segment
  - Duration: 2 weeks minimum (target 200+ conversions per variant)
- **Owner:** GTM Lead + Content Agent (landing page variants)
- **Timeline:** Week 1 (2026-04-28 execution start) | Results by 2026-05-12
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED

#### ASSUMPTION #2: Marketing Managers (End Users) are Drowning in Manual Reporting
- **Assumption Statement:** The target end user (Marketing Manager, age 30-45) spends 6+ hours/week on manual report compilation, and this pain point is their #1 motivation to adopt ReportEase.
- **Why It Matters:** If agencies don't actually spend this time, time-savings messaging fails, and positioning pivots to accuracy/ease of use. Entire value prop hierarchy collapses.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - Anecdotal feedback from 2 beta user interviews
  - Industry assumption based on consultant blogs (Forrester, Gartner)
  - No direct quantified data from target segment
- **Validation Method:** Survey 50+ Marketing Managers in target agencies (ScoutHQ, Hunter.io list); ask: "How many hours/week do you spend on manual report compilation?" Segment answers by agency size. Cross-check with time-tracking data if available.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - Median time spent on reporting is 5-8 hours/week (validates 6hr claim within ±2 hours)
  - >70% identify reporting as "top 3 time-consuming tasks"
  - >60% mention accuracy/errors as secondary pain point
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - Online survey (Typeform) deployed to LinkedIn messaging to 50 agency marketing managers
  - Questions: (1) hours/week on reporting, (2) top 3 time sinks, (3) frequency of data-entry errors, (4) satisfaction with current tools (1-10 scale)
  - Segments: small (10-20 people), medium (20-50 people)
  - Target: 20+ completed responses per segment
- **Owner:** Research Agent + Outreach Agent (survey deployment)
- **Timeline:** Week 1 (2026-04-28 deployment) | Results by 2026-05-05
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED

#### ASSUMPTION #3: CMO/VP Operations is the Economic Buyer and Primary Decision-Maker
- **Assumption Statement:** Budget decisions and vendor selection are made by CMO or VP Operations (not Marketing Manager or agency owner). This person prioritizes ROI and team productivity over individual ease-of-use.
- **Why It Matters:** If agency owner is the buyer, sales approach changes (1-2 week cycle vs. 4-week committee). If PM makes decisions, messaging should emphasize ease-of-use over productivity ROI.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - Company persona definition assumes CMO/VP Ops
  - No validation from actual agency decision-makers
  - HubSpot marketplace shows "Admin" as typical buyer, but doesn't clarify seniority
- **Validation Method:** Send outreach emails to 30 agency CMOs/VP Ops and 30 agency owners; measure response rate, question-type patterns, and trial conversion rate. Conduct phone interviews with 5-10 actual buyers from 9-customer list to understand buying committee and approval process.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - VP Ops/CMO segment responds at >25% rate (vs. <15% for other titles)
  - In phone calls, 80%+ confirm "VP or above" is required for budget approval
  - Initial trial sign-ups correlate with VP/CMO outreach (not PM-level)
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - Cold email campaign (Apollo): 30 emails to CMO/VP Ops titles at target agencies, 30 to agency owners
  - Subject line: "Quick question about your reporting process" (neutral, not promotional)
  - Measure: open rate, reply rate, question themes, trial click rate
  - Follow-up: schedule 10-minute calls with 5-10 responders; ask: (1) Who makes vendor decisions? (2) What's your approval process? (3) Timeline to decision? (4) Would you try a free trial?
- **Owner:** Outreach Agent + GTM Lead (email campaign + phone calls)
- **Timeline:** Week 1 (2026-04-28 deployment) | Preliminary data by 2026-05-05, phone interviews complete by 2026-05-12
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED

#### ASSUMPTION #4: Agencies Will Pay $1,200/Year Per User
- **Assumption Statement:** The target customer will willingly pay $1,200/year (~$100/month) per user for ReportEase. This is based on competitor pricing (HubSpot, Looker, etc.) and perceived value. Agencies view this as justified ROI (6 hrs/week saved = ~$3,000/year per user).
- **Why It Matters:** Pricing assumption directly impacts LTV and revenue projections. If willingness-to-pay is $600 instead of $1,200, LTV drops 50%, and unit economics fail. A 3-month pricing correction would leave $500K+ in foregone revenue on table.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - HubSpot marketplace lists reporting tools at $99-300/month
  - Looker Studio is free (but limited)
  - Assumption that 6 hours/week × $50/hour (loaded labor cost) = $3,000/year benefit justifies $1,200 price
  - No direct pricing validation from target segment
- **Validation Method:** Landing page A/B pricing test (3 variants at different price points). Measure CTR, trial sign-up conversion rate, and forward to trial (funnel drop-off). Survey 30+ target agencies on pricing sensitivity.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - Landing page variant at $1,200/year converts at >2% rate (form submission)
  - <20% increase in conversion rate at $99/month (suggests price elasticity is low)
  - >50% of surveyed agencies confirm $1,200 is "acceptable" or "fair" price for the value
  - Trial-to-paid conversion rate is >15% (as proxy for perceived value)
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - Landing page A/B test: 3 variants, each highlighting different price point
    - Variant A: "$99/month (Annual: $1,188)" → focuses on monthly framing
    - Variant B: "$1,200/year (~ $100/month)" → annual frame
    - Variant C: "$149/month (Annual: $1,788)" → premium frame, emphasizes ROI calculator
  - Metric: Form submission rate, trial sign-up CPC, trial conversion rate
  - Duration: 2 weeks (target 300+ clicks per variant)
  - Parallel survey: Typeform to 30 agencies, ask "Would you pay $1,200/year for a tool that saves your team 6 hours/week? Why/why not? What price would feel right?"
- **Owner:** GTM Lead + Content Agent (landing page), Research Agent (survey)
- **Timeline:** Week 1 (2026-04-28 deployment) | Results by 2026-05-12
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED

#### ASSUMPTION #5: Sales Cycle is 2-4 Weeks
- **Assumption Statement:** From initial outreach to trial sign-up, sales cycle is 2-4 weeks. From trial sign-up to paid conversion, additional 1-2 weeks. Total: 3-6 weeks from first touch to closed deal.
- **Why It Matters:** Sales cycle length drives CAC. If cycle is actually 8-12 weeks, CAC doubles, hurting LTV:CAC ratio. Forecasting, hiring, and burn rate projections depend on this assumption.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - HubSpot reports typical SaaS sales cycles for SMB segment are 2-6 weeks
  - Beta feedback from 2 agencies suggests "quick" decision process, but no formal timeline
  - No tracking of actual sign-up-to-paid dates from MVBP
- **Validation Method:** Implement salesforce/CRM-style tracking on MVBP; record: (1) date of first outreach, (2) trial sign-up date, (3) paid subscription date, (4) associated email/company. After 20+ conversions, calculate median and percentile distribution.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - Median time from outreach to trial: 10-21 days (validates "2-4 weeks")
  - Median time from trial to paid: 7-14 days
  - >80% of conversions complete within 6-week window
  - No "long tail" of >60-day sales cycles (would indicate hidden friction)
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - Add tracking fields to MVBP sign-up form and trial/paid conversion pages
  - Data captured: email, company, first_outreach_date, trial_signup_date, paid_start_date
  - Duration: Run for 4-8 weeks of active customer acquisition (track minimum 20 sign-ups)
  - Analysis: Calculate percentiles, segment by acquisition channel (email, ads, web)
- **Owner:** Analytics Lead + Dev Lead (MVBP instrumentation)
- **Timeline:** Implement by 2026-05-05 | Collect data continuously; first report after 4 weeks (2026-06-02)
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED

### CATEGORY 2: PRODUCT ASSUMPTIONS

#### ASSUMPTION #6: Interactive Dashboards Are a Must-Have (Not Nice-to-Have)
- **Assumption Statement:** Target customers require interactive, real-time dashboards to gain value from ReportEase. Without dashboards, the product is "just a prettier Excel report." Dashboards are the #1 feature that justifies the premium over manual reporting or Looker Studio.
- **Why It Matters:** Dashboard development is 40% of MVBP effort. If customers don't value it or prefer static reports, development priorities pivot, timeline shortens 6+ weeks, and feature roadmap resets.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - Product strategy document lists "interactive dashboards" as value prop #3
  - Competitor analysis (Looker, DataStudio) shows they are dashboard-centric
  - No user research on whether agencies actually use dashboards or just export PDFs
- **Validation Method:** Feature usage tracking on MVBP; measure: (1) % of trial users who access dashboard (vs. just reports), (2) time spent in dashboard, (3) % who upgrade to paid after using dashboard vs. those who only view reports. Compare to static report engagement.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - >70% of trial users access the dashboard at least once
  - Dashboard users spend >5 minutes interacting (indicating engagement)
  - Dashboard users convert to paid at >20% rate (vs. <10% for report-only users)
  - In post-trial surveys, >60% cite "dashboard functionality" as reason for upgrade
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - MVBP includes two paths: (A) traditional static report PDF download, (B) interactive dashboard with drill-down, filtering, date-range selection
  - Randomly assign 50% of trial users to each path (via feature flag)
  - Track: conversion rate per path, feature usage time, survey feedback
  - Duration: 4 weeks (minimum 50 trial conversions per path)
- **Owner:** Product Head + Analytics Lead (MVBP instrumentation)
- **Timeline:** Implement feature flag by 2026-05-05 | Results by 2026-06-02
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED (dependent on MVBP deployment)

- **Experiment Design:**

  - Post-trial survey (Typeform): 5-point importance ranking for: (1) Scheduling, (2) Data accuracy, (3) Customization, (4) Dashboard interactivity, (5) Integration breadth

#### ASSUMPTION #8: Real-Time Data Syncing is Valued by Customers
- **Assumption Statement:** Customers require real-time (or near-real-time, <5 min) data syncing from sources like Google Analytics, HubSpot, and ad platforms. This is a key differentiator vs. competitors and justifies premium positioning.
- **Why It Matters:** Real-time syncing requires persistent background jobs, increased infrastructure cost, and higher complexity. If most customers only need daily syncing (adequate for weekly reports), this feature can be descoped, reducing infrastructure cost by 20-30%.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - Product spec assumes real-time syncing is critical
  - No validation from actual users on sync frequency preference
  - Infrastructure cost for real-time syncing is ~$800/month (vs. $300/month for daily)
- **Validation Method:** Feature tracking on MVBP; measure: (1) frequency of data refreshes users trigger, (2) time-of-day for report generation, (3) complaint/feedback about stale data. Survey 20 users on optimal sync frequency.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - Users trigger refresh >3x per day on average (indicates real-time desire)
  - <10% mention "stale data" or "delay" in feedback
  - Survey shows 70%+ prefer "real-time or hourly" over "daily"
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - MVBP offers both manual refresh button and automatic background sync (hourly)
  - Track: refresh button click frequency, user session times relative to last sync
  - Survey: "If we offered 3 sync options—real-time, daily, or weekly—which would you choose? Why?"
- **Owner:** Product Head + Dev Lead
- **Timeline:** Implement tracking by 2026-05-05 | Results by 2026-06-02
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED (dependent on MVBP deployment)

### CATEGORY 3: BUSINESS MODEL & UNIT ECONOMICS ASSUMPTIONS

#### ASSUMPTION #9: LTV:CAC Ratio Will Be ≥ 3:1
- **Assumption Statement:** Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) will be at least 3x Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC). Assuming 3-year average customer life, annual revenue of $1,200/user, 90% retention, and CAC of $400/customer → LTV:CAC = ~$3,240:$400 = 8:1 (healthy).
- **Why It Matters:** LTV:CAC ratio <3:1 is unsustainable; unit economics fail. If actual CAC is $800 or LTV is $1,620, ratio drops to 2:1, requiring immediate repricing, efficiency cuts, or market pivot.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - CAC estimate of $400 is based on: ($50k sales/marketing budget / 125 customers) ÷ 3 months = rough estimate
  - LTV assumes: $1,200 annual × 2.7 year avg lifetime (90% annual retention) × 30% gross margin = $972 lifetime profit
  - These are ESTIMATES, not validated from real spend
- **Validation Method:** Track actual CAC by channel (email, ads, content, partnerships) across first 50-100 paying customers. Calculate actual LTV from cohort analysis after 12 months (track retention, churn, expansion revenue). Compare to projections.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - Actual CAC ≤ $500 per customer across all channels (within 25% of estimate)
  - 12-month churn rate ≤ 5% per month (validates LTV assumptions)
  - LTV:CAC ratio ≥ 3:1 by month 9 (after first cohort matures)
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - Implement UTM tagging on all acquisition channels (email, Google Ads, organic, partnerships)
  - Track: acquisition channel, channel cost, customer sign-up date, revenue per customer
  - Set up cohort retention analysis in analytics (measure cohort survival % at 30, 60, 90, 180 days)
  - Monthly reporting: CAC by channel, LTV projection by cohort, ratio trending
- **Owner:** Analytics Lead + GTM Lead
- **Timeline:** Start tracking immediately (2026-04-28) | Preliminary analysis at 2026-07-28 (3 months data), full analysis at 2026-10-28 (6+ months)
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED

#### ASSUMPTION #10: Annual Churn Rate Will Be <5% Per Month
- **Assumption Statement:** Monthly churn (customers canceling subscription) will be <5% per month. For a 100-customer base, this means 95 customers remain each month. This assumption is critical to LTV projections and SaaS metrics.
- **Why It Matters:** If actual churn is 10%/month, LTV halves, and unit economics collapse. A 2-month delay in validating churn could mean discovering the business model is broken 60+ days after launch.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - SaaS industry benchmark for SMB segment is 3-8% monthly churn
  - No product-market fit signals yet, so churn could be higher (10-15%) initially
  - Churn will likely improve as product is refined and customer success improves
- **Validation Method:** Track trial sign-ups and conversions to paid; then track subscription cancellations monthly. Measure: (1) month-over-month churn %, (2) reasons for churn (survey), (3) cohort survival curves.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - Monthly churn stabilizes <5% after first 3 cohorts (month 4 onwards)
  - 90-day survival rate >70% (3 months of 95% retention = 86%)
  - Leading indicators (NPS, feature usage, engagement) show positive trend for low-churn cohorts
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - MVBP tracks subscription status: trial_start_date, paid_start_date, cancellation_date
  - Monthly cohort analysis: Track each month's sign-ups and measure their survival at 30, 60, 90+ days
  - Churn survey (email to canceled customers): "Why did you cancel? What could we improve?"
  - Leading metrics to watch: feature adoption rate, dashboard views/week, scheduled reports active
- **Owner:** Analytics Lead + Product Head (retention analysis + customer success)
- **Timeline:** Start tracking at day 1 (2026-04-28) | First meaningful data at 2026-06-28 (2 months), reliable churn signal at 2026-07-28 (3 months)
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED

#### ASSUMPTION #11: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Will Be <$400 Per Customer
- **Assumption Statement:** Through a mix of channels (email outreach, content marketing, paid ads, partnerships), CAC will remain below $400. This is critical to LTV:CAC ratio and unit economics. Assumes blended cost across channels.
- **Why It Matters:** If actual CAC is $600+, the business model is broken. A 4-week delay in discovering high CAC means overspending and capital burn without course correction.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - Email outreach has near-zero variable cost (tools ~$50/month for 500 sends)
  - Google Ads for SMB SaaS typical CPC is $2-5; estimated 2% conversion = $100-250 CPA
  - Content/organic: zero cost but high time investment
  - Estimate: blended CAC ~$400 (rough estimate from budget allocation)
- **Validation Method:** Implement UTM tracking on all channels; calculate CAC = (total acquisition spend / customers acquired) by channel. Refine CAC weekly as more customers convert.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - Email CAC <$50 (near zero) — validates high-volume, low-cost channel
  - Google Ads CAC <$300 — acceptable for paid channel
  - Blended CAC (all channels) <$400 by month 3
  - No single channel exceeds CAC budget thresholds
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - Set up dashboard tracking: [Channel] [Spend] [Sign-ups] [CAC]
  - Channels: Email, Google Ads, Organic, Content, Partnerships, Referrals
  - Weekly reporting: Which channels are <$400 CAC? Which are above?
  - Optimize: kill channels >$500 CAC; double down on <$250 CAC
- **Owner:** GTM Lead + Analytics Lead
- **Timeline:** Start tracking immediately (2026-04-28) | Weekly reporting begins 2026-05-05
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED

### CATEGORY 4: MESSAGING & POSITIONING ASSUMPTIONS

#### ASSUMPTION #12: Time-Savings Messaging Resonates Most with Target Audience
- **Assumption Statement:** Among the three value propositions (Time savings, Accuracy, Visualization), "Time savings" messaging will generate the highest engagement and conversion rate. This is why it's positioned as value prop #1.
- **Why It Matters:** If this assumption is wrong, messaging, landing page copy, and ad creative all pivot. A 3-week messaging correction could mean 20-30% higher CTR and 15-25% higher conversion.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - Persona research suggests Marketing Managers are time-starved (anecdotal)
  - Assumption: 6 hrs/week saved = $3,000/year value
  - No A/B testing of messaging effectiveness
- **Validation Method:** Landing page A/B test with three headline variants, each emphasizing one of the three value props. Measure: CTR, form submission rate, trial conversion rate.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - Time-savings headline converts at >2% rate (form submission)
  - Time-savings converts at >1.3x rate of accuracy headline
  - Time-savings converts at >1.3x rate of visualization headline
  - Ad engagement (CTR) follows same pattern
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - Three landing page variants, identical except hero headline + subheadline:
    - Variant A (Control): "Reclaim 6 Hours Every Week | Automated Marketing Reports"
    - Variant B (Accuracy): "Zero Data-Entry Errors | Automate Your Marketing Reports"
    - Variant C (Visualization): "Interactive Dashboards for Instant Insights | Automated Marketing Reports"
  - Split traffic equally 33%/33%/33% for 2 weeks
  - Measure: CTR from ads, form submission %, trial conversion %
  - Launch via Google Ads (cost <$100 for test)
- **Owner:** Content Agent + GTM Lead (landing page + ads)

#### ASSUMPTION #14: "Free Trial" CTA Converts Better Than "Learn More"
- **Assumption Statement:** A clear "Start Free Trial" call-to-action will convert at a higher rate than softer CTAs like "Learn More," "Get Demo," or "Schedule Call." Direct trial access reduces friction.
- **Why It Matters:** CTA copy directly impacts conversion rate. A better-converting CTA could improve form submission rate by 25-50%, accelerating customer acquisition and reducing CAC.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - SaaS best practices favor direct trial CTAs
  - No A/B testing on ReportEase landing pages
- **Validation Method:** A/B test four CTA variants on landing page. Measure: click rate, form submission rate, trial sign-up completion rate.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - "Start Free Trial" CTA converts at ≥1.3x rate of "Learn More" CTA
  - Form submission rate increases by 15%+ with trial CTA
  - Trial-to-paid conversion ≥10% (proxy for intent quality)
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - Variant A (Control): "Learn More" button (gray, secondary)
  - Variant B (Primary): "Start Free Trial" button (green, primary)
  - Variant C: "Try for Free – No Credit Card Required" (removes friction)
  - Variant D: "Get Instant Access to Demo" (emphasizes speed)
  - 2-week test; measure button click rate, form completion, trial conversion
- **Owner:** GTM Lead + Content Agent
- **Timeline:** Launch by 2026-04-28 | Results by 2026-05-12

#### ASSUMPTION #15: Pricing Sensitivity is Low (Demand Inelastic)
- **Assumption Statement:** Agency customers are relatively insensitive to price within the $99-149/month range. A 50% price increase ($99 → $149) will reduce demand by <20%, indicating low price elasticity and high willingness to pay based on perceived value.
- **Why It Matters:** Price elasticity determines optimal pricing and revenue upside. If demand is highly elastic, pricing strategy shifts to volume + retention. If inelastic, premium pricing is sustainable.
- **Current Evidence:** 
  - Comparable tools (HubSpot reporting add-on) cost $99-200/month
  - TAM calculation assumes $1,200/year willingness to pay
  - No validated pricing elasticity data
- **Validation Method:** Landing page A/B pricing test (3 price points). Measure: form submission rate, trial sign-up rate, and calculate elasticity.
- **Success Criteria:** 
  - $99/month variant: 2.0% form submission rate
  - $149/month variant: >1.6% form submission rate (20% drop, acceptable)
  - $199/month variant: >1.2% form submission rate (40% drop, indicates elasticity)
  - If $149 converts at <1.2%, price is above willingness-to-pay ceiling
- **Experiment Design:** 
  - Three landing page variants, identical except pricing section:
    - Variant A: $99/month (annual: $1,188)
    - Variant B: $149/month (annual: $1,788)
    - Variant C: $199/month (annual: $2,388)
  - 2-week test; 33% traffic split per variant
  - Measure: CTR, form submission %, trial sign-up conversion %
  - Calculate: (Conv Rate B / Conv Rate A) and (Conv Rate C / Conv Rate A)
- **Owner:** GTM Lead + Analytics Lead
- **Timeline:** Launch by 2026-04-28 | Results by 2026-05-12
- **Current Status:** NOT STARTED

### High-Impact Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation | Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Assumption #1 fails: Enterprise is better market | Medium | Critical | Parallel targeting of enterprise; test early | Agency converts at <2x enterprise rate |
| Assumption #4 fails: Pricing is too high | High | Critical | Run pricing sensitivity test in Week 1; have $99/month fallback | <15% submit form at $1,200 |

| Assumption #9 fails: Unit economics broken | High | Critical | Track CAC weekly; optimize channels aggressively | CAC >$600 by Week 8 |
| Assumption #10 fails: Churn is 10%+/month | Medium | Critical | Improve onboarding and customer success urgently | >6% churn by Week 12 |

### Contingency Actions

**If Assumption #1 Fails (Beachhead is Wrong):**
- Pivot targeting to enterprise segment or in-house teams
- Extend sales cycle budget from 3-6 weeks to 8-12 weeks
- Increase CAC allowance to $800-1000
- Adjust pricing upward (enterprise will pay more)

**If Assumption #4 Fails (Pricing Too High):**
- Drop to $99/month immediately
- Increase volume targets to maintain LTV
- Recalculate LTV:CAC ratio; may need to cut CAC or find expansion revenue

**If Assumption #2 Fails (Time Pain Overstated):**
- Pivot positioning to accuracy or ease-of-use
- Highlight elimination of data-entry errors instead
- Simplify product to focus on most impactful feature (accuracy)

**If Assumption #9 Fails (Unit Economics Broken):**
- Immediately audit and optimize acquisition channels
- Kill channels with CAC >$500
- Negotiate better rates on paid ads
- Increase pricing to raise LTV (but test first)
- Consider product-led growth (viral/referral) to reduce CAC
