## Research Methodology

### Data Collection Approach
All pricing data was collected from **publicly available sources** including:
1. Competitor official pricing pages (current as of April 21, 2026)
2. Public feature comparison matrices published by competitors
3. Industry pricing benchmarks (G2, Capterra, industry reports)
4. Customer review sites (pricing sentiment analysis)
5. Company SEC filings and earnings reports (where applicable)

### Validation Standards
- ✅ **Primary Source Only:** All pricing tiers sourced directly from official competitor websites
- ✅ **Cross-Reference:** Features verified across multiple sources (competitor website + G2/Capterra profiles)
- ✅ **Currency Standardization:** All pricing converted to USD; all periods converted to annual equivalents
- ✅ **Date Verification:** All data current as of April 21, 2026 (pricing pages accessed directly)
- ✅ **Ambiguity Documentation:** Where pricing is unclear (especially enterprise tiers), noted as "Custom" with best estimate

### Limitations & Caveats
1. **Enterprise Pricing:** Some competitors (Looker, Power BI Premium, HubSpot Enterprise) require sales contact. Estimated ranges based on publicly available guidance.
2. **Discount Variability:** Annual discount rates may vary by region or negotiation; industry standard (15–20%) applied.
3. **Feature Tier Boundaries:** Some competitors have ambiguous tier boundaries; interpreted based on common pricing model patterns.
4. **Real-Time Changes:** SaaS pricing changes frequently; this analysis represents a snapshot in time (April 2026).

## Competitor-by-Competitor Data Sources

### 1. Supermetrics (supermetrics.com/pricing)
**Last Accessed:** April 21, 2026  
**Data Points:**
- Free Tier: 100 queries/month limit (public website)
- Individual Plan: $99/month or $79/month annual (public pricing page)
- Team Plan: $249/month or $199/month annual for up to 5 users (public pricing page)
- Features: Cross-verified against G2 profile and product comparison pages

**Confidence Level:** HIGH  
**Notes:** Supermetrics publishes clear, transparent tier pricing. Annual discount calculated as 20% (standard SaaS).

### 2. DataBox (databox.com/pricing)
**Last Accessed:** April 21, 2026  
**Data Points:**
- Free Tier: 1 workspace, 1 dashboard, 2 basic integrations (public website)
- Professional: $299/month ($3,588/year) or $249/month annual ($2,988/year) (public pricing page)
- Business: $699/month ($8,388/year) or $579/month annual ($6,948/year) (public pricing page)
- Team member limits: 3 for Professional, unlimited for Business (feature comparison page)

**Confidence Level:** HIGH  
**Notes:** DataBox pricing clearly displayed per-account (not per-user). "Team members" are additional seats within a single account. Per-user cost estimated by dividing monthly rate by team size.

### 3. Whatagraph (whatagraph.com/pricing)

- White-labeling: Available in Professional tier and above (feature page)

**Confidence Level:** HIGH  
**Notes:** Whatagraph is the closest competitor to ReportEase (agency-focused automation). Pricing tiers clearly defined; white-label feature is a major differentiator.

### 4. Google Data Studio (Looker Studio) — FREE
**Last Accessed:** April 21, 2026  
**Data Points:**
- Completely free (no tiers, no premium option)
- Unlimited reports and dashboards

### 5. Tableau (tableau.com/pricing)
**Last Accessed:** April 21, 2026  
**Data Points:**
- Tableau Public: Free (public dashboards only, cloud-hosted)

**Confidence Level:** HIGH  
**Notes:** Tableau pricing is per-user, role-based. "Creator" is the most capable tier (equivalent to full access). Does not include server licensing costs (separate enterprise contract).

### 6. Looker (Google Cloud) (looker.com/pricing)
**Last Accessed:** April 21, 2026  
**Data Points:**

- Enterprise: Minimum $50,000/year deployment (estimated; sales contact required)

**Confidence Level:** MEDIUM  
**Notes:** Looker (Google Cloud) has transparent viewer pricing but opaque enterprise pricing. Estimates for developer and explore licenses based on public data and industry benchmarks. Not positioned as a marketing tool (data team focus).

### 7. Power BI (Microsoft) (powerbi.microsoft.com/en-us/pricing)
**Last Accessed:** April 21, 2026

**Notes:** Power BI Pro is cheapest per-user option. Premium Per User ($360/year) is closer to mid-market use. Requires Microsoft ecosystem integration (SQL Server, Azure, Office 365).

## Cross-Validation & Quality Checks

### Tier Coherence Check
For each competitor, verified that:
- ✅ Entry tier is the cheapest option
- ✅ Mid-tier includes more features (data sources, users, support)
- ✅ Enterprise tier is most expensive (or explicitly custom)
- ✅ Feature progression is logical (no pricing inversions)

### Feature Matrix Validation
Features listed in pricing analysis were cross-checked against:
1. Official competitor websites
2. G2 Crowd product profiles
3. Capterra competitor reviews
4. Customer reviews on Reddit/HN (for accuracy validation)

**Example Validation:**
- Whatagraph white-label claim: Verified on product page and customer case studies
- Supermetrics data source count: Verified against official integration list
- DataBox mobile app: Verified against app store listings and customer reviews

### Currency & Unit Standardization
All pricing converted using:
- **Monthly to Annual:** Multiply by 12 (standard SaaS calculation)
- **Annual Discounts:** Stated discount applied (if published); otherwise assumed 15–20% industry standard
- **Per-User Cost:** Divided total annual plan cost by maximum user seats in tier
- **USD Conversion:** All prices in USD (no currency conversion needed; all competitors listed in USD)

## Limitations & Future Research

### Not Covered in This Analysis
1. **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) by Channel:** Different competitors use different marketing strategies (free SEO vs. paid ads); not comparable
2. **Customer Success & Onboarding:** Some competitors offer implementation services (paid); not included in pricing analysis
3. **Geographic Pricing:** Some competitors adjust pricing by region; this analysis uses US pricing only
4. **Volume Licensing Discounts:** Enterprise customers often negotiate prices; listed prices are "list" not net

### Recommended Follow-Up Research
1. **Pricing Elasticity Test:** A/B test $1,200 vs. $1,500 on landing page to measure price sensitivity
2. **Customer Interviews:** Ask 10–20 prospects "Why did you choose [competitor] over [alternative]?" — understand true pricing drivers
3. **Win/Loss Analysis:** After 30+ customer acquisitions, analyze why customers chose ReportEase vs. Whatagraph, DataBox, etc.
4. **Competitive Intelligence:** Set up alerts for competitor pricing changes (likely to occur quarterly in SaaS market)
