## 4. CONVERSION TRACKING SETUP

### Conversion Event: Trial Sign-Up

**Tracking Method:** Google Analytics 4 Integration  
**Event Name:** "trial_signup"  
**Trigger:** When user submits form with email and clicks "Start Trial" button on landing page

**Implementation Details:**
1. Landing page includes hidden UTM parameters from Google Ads:
   - `utm_source=google`
   - `utm_medium=cpc`
   - `utm_campaign=reportease_search_2026_04_21`
   - `utm_content=[ad_variant]` (time_savings, accuracy, or visualization)

2. Form submission triggers Google Analytics event:
```javascript
gtag('event', 'trial_signup', {
  'campaign': 'reportease_search_2026_04_21',
  'ad_variant': '[variant_id]',
  'form_completion_step': 'form_submitted',
  'event_category': 'engagement',
  'event_label': 'trial_signup_form'
});
```

3. Conversion imported into Google Ads via Google Analytics 4 link
4. Conversion window: 30 days (default)
5. Conversion attribution: Last click (default for search campaigns)

### Secondary Metrics to Track
- **Impressions:** Ad visibility
- **Clicks:** Click-through rate (CTR) benchmark
- **Cost per Click (CPC):** Bid efficiency
- **Click-through Rate (CTR):** Ad relevance signal (target > 3%)
- **Conversion Rate:** Trial sign-ups ÷ clicks
- **Cost per Conversion (CPC):** Actual CPA vs. target CPA of $50
- **Quality Score:** Ad + landing page relevance (target: 7+)

## 10. TROUBLESHOOTING & CONTINGENCIES

### Issue: Quality Score < 6
**Root Causes:** Ad relevance low, landing page slow, poor CTR  
**Solutions:**
- Revise headlines/descriptions to match keyword more closely
- Test landing page load time (target < 2s)
- Ensure landing page headline matches ad headline
- Check mobile UX (critical for mobile traffic)

### Issue: CPA > $100
**Root Causes:** Low conversion rate, high CPC, landing page friction  
**Solutions:**
- Review landing page form fields (too many? unclear CTAs?)
- A/B test form with fewer required fields
- Audit landing page copy for clarity
- Check if visitors are qualified (right company size, industry)
- Consider excluding low-intent keywords (free, template, guide)

### Issue: Very High CPC ($1.50+)
**Root Causes:** High competition, low Quality Score, bid strategy too aggressive  
**Solutions:**
- Shift to Phrase Match from Broad Match (more control)
- Reduce target CPA to lower bid cap
- Focus budget on lower-CPC keywords
- Consider adding long-tail keywords with less competition

### Issue: No Conversions After Day 2
**Root Causes:** Landing page UX issue, form error, unqualified traffic  
**Solutions:**
- Test landing page form end-to-end
- Check GA4 conversion event firing (use GA4 debug mode)
- Review click sources (ensure it's actual interested users)
- Pause underperforming keywords immediately
- Check email deliverability (trial confirmation emails going to spam?)

## 11. BUDGET & FINANCIAL MODEL

### Campaign Budget Allocation
- **Daily Budget:** $25 USD
- **Campaign Duration:** 20 days
- **Total Budget:** $500 USD
- **Bid Strategy:** Maximize Conversions (target CPA $50)

### Unit Economics Validation
**Assumptions:**
- LTV = $1,200/year (annual subscription revenue per user)
- 5-year customer lifetime = $6,000 total profit
- Target LTV:COCA ratio = 3:1
- Max allowable CAC = $6,000 ÷ 3 = $2,000 per customer
- Conservative first-year CAC target = $500 per customer (keeps LTV:COCA ratio at 12:1)

**Campaign Validation:**
- If target CPA = $50, and 10 sign-ups convert to paying customers (100% conversion):
  - Total CAC = $50 × 10 = $500
  - LTV per customer = $1,200 first year × 5 years avg lifetime = $6,000
  - LTV:COCA = $6,000 ÷ $500 = **12:1** ✓ (exceeds 3:1 target)
  
**Economic Conclusion:**
At $50 CPA and $1,200 ARR pricing, the campaign ROI is highly favorable even at conservative conversion assumptions.
