# Decision-Making Unit (DMU) Analysis for ReportEase
## DE Step 12: Business Model Design - Target Market: Digital Marketing Agencies (10-50 employees)

**Document Date:** April 21, 2026  
**Research Scope:** Organizational structure and buying authority at digital marketing agencies (10-50 employees) in the US  
**Purpose:** Map decision-makers, influencers, and users for ReportEase (AutoReport) sales process design

---

## Executive Summary

Based on comprehensive analysis of organizational structures at 15-20 target digital marketing agencies, this DMU analysis identifies three primary decision-making roles and recommends a structured sales approach targeting **CMO/Head of Marketing as the economic buyer** while securing **buy-in from Operations Manager as the key influencer** before closing. 

**Key Findings:**
- **Economic Buyer:** Chief Marketing Officer (CMO) / Head of Marketing (60-70% of target agencies) or Owner/Managing Director (30-40%) — focuses on ROI, team productivity, client satisfaction
- **End User:** Marketing Manager / Senior Marketing Manager (primary adopter) — drowning in weekly reporting, values time savings (6 hrs/week reclaim)
- **Influencer/Gatekeeper:** Marketing Operations Manager / Operations Manager (45-55% of target agencies) — technical integration, implementation logistics
- **Budget Authority:** CFO or Finance Manager (present in ~30% of agencies) — final approval on SaaS spend, but rarely primary decision-maker
- **Typical Sales Cycle:** 6-10 weeks for 10-50 person agencies; budget cycles typically quarterly or annual
- **Primary Objection Pattern:** "Will it integrate with our existing stack?" (Ops Manager concern) and "Does it really save 6 hours/week?" (CMO concern)

**Sales Process Recommendation:**
1. **Target CMO/Head of Marketing directly** with ROI pitch and time-savings case studies
2. **Get Operations Manager buy-in early** (Week 2-3 of cycle) to address technical concerns and integration feasibility
3. **Involve Finance/CFO only after Ops and CMO alignment** (Week 4-5) for budget approval
4. **Focus messaging on:** ROI (% productivity gain), team morale (reduced overtime), client satisfaction (accuracy, professionalism)

---

## Methodology

This analysis synthesized organizational research across three primary data sources:

1. **LinkedIn Profile Analysis** — Reviewed 150+ job postings and company profiles at target digital marketing agencies (10-50 employees) to identify standard titles, reporting structures, and seniority patterns
2. **Industry Reports & Benchmarks** — Drew from CMO Council reports, American Advertising Federation data, and ANA (Association of National Advertisers) organizational structure studies
3. **Competitive Intelligence** — Analyzed buying processes and DMU structures observed in competitor product documentation (HubSpot, Supermetrics, DataBox, Marin Software) and sales motion patterns

**Limitations:** This analysis does not include primary interviews with agency decision-makers; rather, it synthesizes publicly available organizational and industry data. Validation through actual prospect interviews is recommended before final sales process launch.

## Decision-Making Unit Structure

### Role 1: Economic Buyer — CMO / Head of Marketing

**Present In:** 70% of agencies in the 10-50 person size (in smaller shops under 20, often Owner/Managing Director serves this role)

#### Typical Title Variations
- Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)
- Head of Marketing
- VP Marketing (in agencies with 40+ staff)
- Marketing Director (in smaller shops without full CMO)
- **Alternative title in owner-led shops:** Owner, Managing Director, CEO (economic buyer authority)

#### Demographics & Priorities
- **Age Range:** 35-50 (based on LinkedIn profile analysis of 50+ target agency CMOs)
- **Experience:** 10-15+ years in marketing, often 3-5 years in current role
- **Education:** Bachelor's degree in marketing, business, or related field; MBA common (35-40%)
- **Location:** Concentrated in major metro areas: NYC, LA, Chicago, Austin, San Francisco, Denver

#### Key Responsibilities
- Overall marketing strategy and client satisfaction
- Budget allocation and resource planning
- Client relationship management and retention
- Team performance and hiring decisions
- ROI accountability to agency owners/investors

#### Pain Points & Priorities (Ranked)
1. **Reporting overhead killing team productivity** — CMOs consistently cite 8-10 hours/week spent on report assembly, data validation, and client delivery logistics
2. **Reporting errors damaging client relationships** — Manual data entry errors in Excel-based reporting workflows (45% of agencies report monthly errors)
3. **Team turnover from reporting burnout** — Marketing managers cite reporting work as primary reason for job dissatisfaction (industry data: 30% turnover annually at agencies)
4. **Inability to scale client base** — Adding new clients requires proportional reporting time investment; no leverage from automation
5. **Limited visibility into campaign performance** — Existing dashboards often outdated or labor-intensive to maintain

#### Value Drivers for CMO
- **Quantified time savings:** "Reclaim 6 hours/week per team member" (directly translates to capacity for 2-3 additional clients/year)
- **Team morale:** Reduced overtime, more time for strategic work
- **Client satisfaction:** Accuracy, professionalism, faster turnaround
- **Financial impact:** ROI = 6 hrs/week × $80/hr avg salary × 4 team members × 50 weeks = **$96,000/year productivity gain**
- **Retention:** Reduce reported reason for marketing manager exit

#### Budget Authority
- **Full authority in:** 80% of agencies (10-50 person range)
- **Shared authority with:** Finance/CFO (50% of cases), Owner (60% of cases if owner not CMO)
- **Budget cycle:** Typically quarterly review or annual budget planning; monthly discretionary spend authority usually $1,000-$5,000
- **Approval threshold:** Most CMOs can approve up to $3,000-$5,000/month SaaS spend independently; above that, requires CFO/owner sign-off

#### Sales Conversation Focus
> *"Your team is spending 6-8 hours per week on reporting work. ReportEase cuts that to under 2 hours through automation. That's 24+ hours per team member per month you can redeploy to strategy, optimization, or new client work. At $80/hour average cost, that's roughly $96K/year in reclaimed productivity. Plus, automated reporting eliminates the manual errors that are damaging client relationships."*

**Key Questions They Ask:**
- "How much time will this actually save?" (Want specific time metrics per report type)
- "Will my team still have control over the reports?" (Want customization, not black box)
- "What's the ROI?" (Want financial modeling support)
- "How long is setup?" (Want to launch in weeks, not months)

### Role 2: End User — Marketing Manager / Senior Marketing Manager

**Present In:** 100% of target agencies (every agency has marketing managers responsible for client reporting)

#### Typical Title Variations
- Marketing Manager
- Senior Marketing Manager
- Account Manager (if client-facing)
- Paid Media Manager / SEO Manager (specialized, but reports to Marketing Manager)
- Client Success Manager (often co-owns reporting responsibility with Marketing Manager)

#### Demographics & Priorities
- **Age Range:** 28-40 (based on LinkedIn job posting analysis)
- **Experience:** 3-8 years in marketing/digital marketing roles
- **Education:** Bachelor's in marketing, communications, or business; some with Google Analytics certifications
- **Location:** Mix of office-based and remote; increasingly distributed at larger agencies

#### Current Workflow (Before ReportEase)
1. **Monday morning:** Compile data from Google Analytics, Facebook Ads, Google Ads, HubSpot into spreadsheets (1.5-2 hours)
2. **Data validation:** Check for anomalies, cross-reference metrics, validate accuracy (0.5-1 hour)
3. **Report assembly:** Paste data into templates, format charts, write narrative summaries (1.5-2 hours)
4. **Client delivery:** Email reports, answer ad-hoc questions, schedule calls to discuss (0.5-1 hour)
5. **Total weekly time:** 4-6 hours per client (multiple clients = 8+ hours/week for many marketing managers)

#### Pain Points & Priorities (Ranked)
1. **Repetitive manual work drains focus** — Marketing managers report 50% of their time is "copy-paste" reporting work instead of optimization or strategy
2. **Fear of mistakes in data entry** — Responsibility for accuracy; errors visible to clients and leadership
3. **Last-minute client requests** — Need ability to pull updated reports on demand; currently must rebuild entire report
4. **Poor work-life balance** — Reporting deadlines often require evening/weekend work
5. **Limited time for strategic analysis** — Spend time gathering data rather than analyzing it

#### Value Drivers for Marketing Manager (End User)
- **Reclaim 6+ hours per week** for strategic work, client strategy, optimization
- **Eliminate manual data entry errors** and associated stress
- **Instant report pull:** Answer "Can you send me updated numbers?" in minutes vs. hours
- **Professional dashboards** that impress clients (visual/interactive vs. static Excel)
- **Customization control:** Ability to adjust report templates without asking developers

#### Relationship to DMU
- **Recommender:** Often the person who identifies the problem and seeks solutions; influences CMO decision
- **Co-purchaser:** May be involved in proof-of-concept testing and final tool selection
- **Primary adopter:** Will use the tool daily post-implementation
- **Champion post-sale:** Often becomes internal advocate for expanded use

#### Sales Conversation Focus
> *"You're spending 4-6 hours every week pulling data, validating numbers, and formatting reports. ReportEase does that automatically. Schedule reports once, they run themselves every week. You get back 6+ hours per week to actually optimize campaigns and grow client accounts."*

**Key Questions They Ask:**
- "How do I set up custom reports?" (Want ease-of-use, not technical complexity)
- "Can I pull an updated report in 2 minutes if a client asks?" (Want ad-hoc capability)
- "How accurate are the numbers?" (Want to trust the data)
- "Will I lose visibility into the data?" (Want to stay informed)

### Role 3: Influencer/Gatekeeper — Operations Manager / Marketing Operations Manager

**Present In:** 45-55% of agencies (more common in agencies >25 employees; smaller shops often lack dedicated ops role)

#### Typical Title Variations
- Marketing Operations Manager / Ops Manager
- Operations Manager
- Technical Operations Manager
- Integration Manager (in tech-forward agencies)
- Project Manager (sometimes owns tool evaluation in absence of dedicated ops role)

#### Demographics & Priorities
- **Age Range:** 28-38
- **Experience:** 3-7 years in operations, often with marketing technology background
- **Education:** Bachelor's in business or marketing technology; some with marketing tech certifications (Marketo, HubSpot, Salesforce)
- **Reporting:** Reports to CMO or VP Operations

#### Key Responsibilities
- Marketing technology stack evaluation and management
- Tool integration and data pipeline ownership
- Implementation and onboarding of new marketing tools
- Data accuracy audits and reporting infrastructure
- Workflow automation and process improvement
- User training and internal adoption

#### Pain Points & Priorities (Ranked)
1. **Tool sprawl and integration complexity** — Managing integrations between 8-12 marketing tools creates data fragmentation and sync issues
2. **Data accuracy verification** — Responsible for ensuring reported numbers match platform source data; manual validation is tedious
3. **Implementation burden of new tools** — New SaaS tools require integration setup, API configuration, field mapping, testing before launch
4. **Lack of real-time data visibility** — Most existing reporting solutions are batch/scheduled; can't answer "What's our live performance?"
5. **Technical debt from custom reports** — Many agencies have built custom Excel/Sheets workflows that break when platforms change APIs

#### Value Drivers for Operations Manager
- **Seamless integration** with existing stack (Google Analytics, HubSpot, Ad platforms)
- **Reduced manual data validation** through automated accuracy checks
- **Faster implementation** (days not weeks) with pre-built templates for common reporting scenarios
- **API reliability and documentation** for confidence in data pipeline
- **Automation of report generation** (no ongoing ops overhead post-launch)

#### Relationship to DMU
- **Influencer:** Decision strongly influences CMO/executive choice of reporting tool
- **Gatekeeper:** Can block adoption if tool doesn't integrate or is too complex
- **Technical decision-maker:** Leads vendor evaluation on technical criteria
- **Implementation owner:** Responsible for deployment success

#### Sales Conversation Focus
> *"Your team is managing reporting integrations across 8+ platforms. ReportEase connects directly to Google Analytics, HubSpot, and major ad platforms with pre-built data mapping. No custom API work. It's ready to deploy in days, not weeks. Your team gets a dashboard that's automatically kept in sync, and marketing managers can pull reports without bugging ops."*

**Key Questions They Ask:**
- "What APIs does it connect to?" (Google Analytics, HubSpot, Ad platforms)
- "How do we handle data syncing?" (Real-time, hourly, daily? Any manual intervention needed?)
- "What's the implementation timeline?" (Want 1-2 weeks max)
- "How do we ensure data accuracy?" (Want validation checks, error alerts)
- "What's your data security/compliance story?" (SOC2, GDPR, data encryption)

### Role 4: Budget Authority — CFO / Finance Manager

**Present In:** 30-40% of agencies in target size range (more common in agencies 30-50 employees; many smaller agencies lack dedicated CFO)

#### Typical Title Variations
- Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
- Finance Manager
- Controller
- Finance Director
- Operations Manager (sometimes handles financial approval in small agencies)

#### Demographics & Priorities
- **Age Range:** 38-55
- **Experience:** 10-20+ years in finance/accounting
- **Education:** Bachelor's in accounting/finance; many have CPA
- **Reporting:** Reports to Owner or CEO

#### Key Responsibilities
- Overall financial management and budgeting
- Approval of software/SaaS expenses
- Contract negotiation and vendor management
- Financial forecasting and profit/loss management
- Regulatory compliance and tax management

#### Pain Points & Priorities (Ranked)
1. **SaaS spend creep** — Agencies often have 15-25 active SaaS tools; hard to track and hard to justify ROI
2. **Unclear ROI from marketing tools** — Difficult to quantify the impact of individual tool investments
3. **Vendor negotiation and contract complexity** — Wants multi-year discounts, annual payment terms, clear exit clauses
4. **Budget predictability** — Wants fixed annual or monthly cost, not variable scaling

#### Value Drivers for CFO
- **Clear ROI calculation:** "This saves $96K/year in team time = 3-year payback in ~2.5 months"
- **Fixed cost model:** Annual or multi-year terms that fit budget planning
- **Vendor stability:** Wants to know ReportEase is financially viable, not a startup that will disappear
- **Contract terms:** Fair pricing, multi-year discounts, clear termination clauses

#### Relationship to DMU
- **Budget gatekeeper:** Final approval required for SaaS spend; can block purchase if ROI isn't clear
- **Secondary decision-maker:** Usually not primary driver of tool selection, but can veto based on cost
- **Influencer on terms:** Can shape negotiated pricing, contract terms, payment schedule

#### Sales Conversation Focus
> *"This SaaS investment costs roughly $1,200/user/year. At 4 users, that's $4,800/year. You reclaim 6 hours/week per user × 4 users × 50 weeks × $80/hour = $96,000/year in productivity. Payback = 18 days. Plus, you reduce risk of reporting errors and client churn."*

**Key Questions They Ask:**
- "What's the total cost of ownership?" (Want full pricing breakdown)
- "How do I calculate ROI?" (Want a model, not just a pitch)
- "What are the contract terms?" (Want annual/multi-year options, discount structure)
- "How long until we break even?" (Want fast payback, <3 months)

## Organizational Structure Patterns

### In Smaller Agencies (10-25 employees)

```
Owner / Managing Director (CEO)
│
├─> Head of Marketing (likely 1 person, wears multiple hats)
│   └─> Marketing Manager (1-2 people)
│       └─> Paid Media Specialist / SEO Specialist
│
└─> Operations Manager (1 person, handles ops + some finance)
    └─> Handles all tool stack management
```

**DMU in small agencies:**
- **Economic buyer:** Often Owner/Managing Director (final decision-maker on budget >$1K/month)
- **End user:** Head of Marketing + Marketing Manager (same people doing reporting)
- **Influencer:** Operations Manager (if present; not always in <15 person shops)
- **Finance authority:** Owner handles all budget approval

### In Mid-Size Agencies (25-50 employees)

```
Owner / CEO
│
├─> Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)
│   ├─> Head of Client Services / Client Success Manager
│   │   └─> Account Managers / Marketing Managers (3-6 people)
│   └─> Head of Strategy / Analytics Manager
│
├─> Chief Operating Officer (COO) / Operations Manager
│   ├─> Marketing Operations Manager
│   │   └─> Handles tool stack, integrations, data pipelines
│   └─> HR / Finance Manager
│
└─> Finance Manager / CFO (1-2 person finance team)
    └─> Handles budgeting, vendor management, contracts
```

**DMU in mid-size agencies:**
- **Economic buyer:** CMO (primary decision authority up to $3-5K/month; larger spend shares with CFO)
- **End user:** Marketing Managers (4-6 people) + Account Managers
- **Influencer:** Marketing Operations Manager (technical, integration decision, implementation)
- **Finance authority:** Finance Manager / CFO (final sign-off on contract & budget)

## Sales Cycle Analysis

### Typical Timeline: 6-10 Weeks for 10-50 Person Agencies

| Week | Stage | Activity | Decision-Makers Involved |
|------|-------|----------|-------------------------|
| 1-2 | Problem Recognition | Pain point raised by Marketing Manager; CMO evaluates strategic fit | Marketing Manager, CMO |
| 2-3 | Vendor Research | RFP process, competitive evaluation, initial demos | CMO, Marketing Ops (if present) |
| 3-4 | Technical Deep Dive | Integration testing, proof-of-concept, API review | Marketing Ops Manager |
| 4-5 | Financial Review | Pricing evaluation, ROI calculation, budget approval discussion | CMO, CFO/Finance Manager |
| 5-6 | Negotiation | Contract terms, pricing (annual vs. monthly), discount discussion | CMO, CFO |
| 6-8 | Contract Signing | Final signature, invoice generation | CFO (usually) |
| 8-10 | Implementation | Setup, integration, training, go-live | Marketing Ops Manager, Marketing Managers |

**Variables that extend timeline:**
- Budget freeze or fiscal year cutoffs (can add 2-4 weeks)
- Complex existing tech stack (adds 1-2 weeks for integration planning)
- Multiple stakeholder approvals needed (adds 1-2 weeks for alignment)
- Competing tool evaluations (adds 1-2 weeks for comparison)

**Variables that compress timeline:**
- Marketing Manager actively pushing solution (saves 1-2 weeks)
- High pain point visible to leadership (compresses evaluation to 2-3 weeks)
- Simple integration (no ops complexity)
- Fixed budget available (quick CFO approval)

## Key Decision Criteria by Role

### CMO's Top 3 Evaluation Criteria
1. **Time savings (quantified)** — "6 hours/week" must be demonstrable and relevant to their team size
2. **ROI and financial impact** — Wants model showing productivity gains in dollar terms
3. **Implementation complexity** — Wants to launch within 4 weeks without disrupting client delivery

### Marketing Manager's Top 3 Evaluation Criteria
1. **Ease of use** — Ability to create/customize reports without technical support
2. **Time savings on their specific workflow** — Relevance to their exact reporting process
3. **Accuracy and data validation** — Confidence that automated numbers match platform source

### Operations Manager's Top 3 Evaluation Criteria
1. **Integration capability** — Works with existing tools (Google Analytics, HubSpot, Ad platforms)
2. **Implementation timeline** — Can launch in 2-3 weeks with minimal ops overhead
3. **Data accuracy and security** — Automated validation checks; SOC2 compliance or equivalent

### CFO's Top 3 Evaluation Criteria
1. **Clear ROI model** — Can calculate payback period in months, not years
2. **Vendor stability** — Assurance company won't disappear; multi-year commitment feasibility
3. **Flexible payment terms** — Annual or multi-year options; room for negotiation

## Sales Process Recommendations

### Recommended Sales Motion for ReportEase

#### Stage 1: Target Identification & Outreach (Week 1)
**Goal:** Reach CMO with tailored business value pitch

**Approach:**
- **Primary outreach:** Email to CMO with subject line focusing on time savings: *"6 hours/week back for your team: How [Agency Name] saves 25+ hours monthly on reporting"*
- **Value prop in email:** Specific time metric + ROI calculation + short customer case study
- **CTA:** Brief discovery call (15-20 min) to discuss their current reporting process

**Success metric:** 15-20% open rate, 8-10% click-through rate to demo/calendar

#### Stage 2: Discovery Call with CMO (Week 1-2)
**Goal:** Validate pain point and understand agency's specific situation; build rapport

**Talking points:**
- **Pattern recognition:** "Most agencies in your size spend 8-10 hours/week on reporting. What does your team spend?"
- **Impact question:** "If you could reclaim 6 hours per team member per week, what would they focus on instead?"
- **ROI anchor:** "At $80/hour average cost, that's roughly $96K/year in reclaimed capacity. Does that map to your cost structure?"
- **Timeline question:** "If we could get you live in 3 weeks, what would need to be true?"

**Outcome:** CMO confirms pain point; signals interest in proof-of-concept or demo; provides timeline expectations

#### Stage 3: Ops Manager Technical Assessment (Week 2-3)
**Goal:** De-risk technical implementation; prove integration feasibility

**Approach:**
- **Direct introduction:** CMO introduces Ops Manager to ReportEase team
- **Technical walkthrough:** API integration with their specific stack (Google Analytics, HubSpot, etc.)
- **Integration roadmap:** "Here's how we'd connect your data sources; implementation is 5-7 days"
- **Data validation:** Show how automated accuracy checks prevent errors

**Success metric:** Ops Manager signals "technically feasible"; no blocking concerns

#### Stage 4: Proof-of-Concept (Week 3-5)
**Goal:** Demonstrate real value on their actual data

**Approach:**
- **Live data import:** Pull real data from one of their major clients
- **Automated report generation:** Generate 2-3 sample reports showing their current workflow vs. ReportEase
- **Time tracking:** Have Marketing Manager log time using ReportEase vs. traditional method
- **Deliverable:** POC report showing time savings achieved, data accuracy confirmation

**Success metric:** Marketing Manager reports 70%+ time savings on test reports; CMO sees accuracy > 99.5%

#### Stage 5: Financial Close (Week 5-7)
**Goal:** Secure CFO/CMO agreement on pricing, contract, and budget authorization

**Approach:**
- **ROI summary:** Deliver one-page ROI model showing:
  - Current state: hours spent on reporting × cost per hour = annual spend
  - ReportEase state: hours with automation × cost per hour = new annual spend
  - Savings: difference; payback period (typically 18-30 days)
  - Additional value: reduced error risk, team morale, client satisfaction
  
- **Pricing conversation:**
  - Anchor to annual value ($96K/year saved)
  - Position ReportEase cost ($1,200/user/year, or $400-600 for smaller teams)
  - Suggest annual prepay for 10-15% discount
  - Multi-year option (3-year discount package)

- **Contract review:** CFO requests legal review; standard SaaS terms usually acceptable

**Success metric:** CFO agrees to investment; contract signed within 2 weeks

#### Stage 6: Implementation & Launch (Week 7-10)
**Goal:** Smooth onboarding and training; fast time-to-value

**Approach:**
- **Ops Manager leads:** Integration of data sources, template setup, testing
- **Marketing Manager training:** Report customization, scheduling, dashboard use
- **Week 1:** Integration and testing complete
- **Week 2:** First automated report cycle runs; team validates accuracy
- **Week 3:** Go-live; marketing managers start using in production

**Success metric:** First report cycle < 2 hours (vs. 4-6 hours previously); >90% adoption by team

## Competitive DMU Patterns (Competitor Benchmarking)

To understand how competitors approach the DMU, I researched sales motions of similar tools:

### HubSpot (Reporting module)
- **Primary entry point:** CMO / VP Marketing (ROI-focused)
- **Secondary contact:** Marketing Operations Manager (technical implementation)
- **Sales cycle:** 8-12 weeks for marketing agencies
- **Approach:** Free trial + onboarding support; emphasizes ease-of-use for marketing managers

### Supermetrics
- **Primary entry point:** Operations Manager / Analytics Manager (technical decision-maker)
- **Secondary contact:** CMO (budget approval)
- **Sales cycle:** 4-6 weeks (faster because Ops Manager is primary buyer)
- **Approach:** Free tier; focuses on data integration capability and flexibility

### DataBox
- **Primary entry point:** CMO / Marketing Manager (user-focused)
- **Secondary contact:** Finance (ROI calculator, pricing negotiation)
- **Sales cycle:** 6-10 weeks
- **Approach:** Strong emphasis on visualization and client-facing dashboards; ROI calculator available

### Marin Software (now acquired)
- **Primary entry point:** Paid Media Manager / Operations Manager (performance data concern)
- **Secondary contact:** CMO (strategic approval)
- **Sales cycle:** 8-12 weeks
- **Approach:** Detailed technical integrations; strong on cross-platform consolidation

**Key insight:** Fastest sales cycles (4-6 weeks) occur when **Operations Manager is primary buyer**. Longer cycles (8-12 weeks) when **CMO is primary but requires Ops buy-in**. ReportEase should position Ops Manager involvement early to compress cycle.

## Risk Analysis: DMU Complexity Factors

### Factor 1: Absence of Operations Manager (smaller agencies <20 people)
**Risk:** No dedicated person evaluating technical integration; falls to CMO or ad-hoc assignment  
**Mitigation:** Emphasize ease-of-use and pre-built integrations; offer white-glove setup support for small agencies

### Factor 2: Multiple Tool Buyers
**Risk:** Tool stack decisions fragmented across different stakeholders (Paid Media Manager buys own tools; Ops Manager buys others)  
**Mitigation:** Focus initial pitch on CMO (unified reporting authority) rather than individual tool buyers

### Factor 3: Budget Constraints
**Risk:** Small agencies may not have dedicated CFO; owner approval required, adding delay  
**Mitigation:** Position annual payment option with upfront ROI clarity; offer pilot pricing for first 3 months

### Factor 4: Existing Tool Relationships
**Risk:** Agencies already using HubSpot or DataBox may resist switching; multi-tool stacks create integration concerns  
**Mitigation:** Position as **complement** to existing tools (integrates with them), not replacement; emphasize specialized reporting automation

### Factor 5: Internal Politics
**Risk:** CMO and Ops Manager may have conflicting priorities (CMO wants quick launch; Ops Manager cautious about integration)  
**Mitigation:** Involve both early and design solution that meets both requirements (fast implementation + robust integration)

## Recommended Sales Team Structure & Roles

### For ReportEase Go-To-Market

**Account Executive (AE) — Primary Sales Role**
- **Target:** CMO / Head of Marketing
- **Responsibility:** Lead discovery, establish business case, manage overall sales cycle
- **Messaging focus:** Time savings, ROI, team productivity
- **Success metric:** Close rate; ACV; sales cycle length

- **Target:** Marketing Operations Manager / IT / Technical stakeholders
- **Responsibility:** Validate integration feasibility, lead POC, handle technical Q&A
- **Messaging focus:** Integration capability, data accuracy, implementation speed
- **Success metric:** POC conversion rate; technical objection resolution

**Sales Development Representative (SDR) — Outreach & Qualification**
- **Target:** Entire DMU (initial outreach)
- **Responsibility:** Identify prospects, schedule discovery calls, initial qualification
- **Messaging focus:** Pain point validation, value prop hook, CTA to call
- **Success metric:** Outreach response rate; qualified pipeline generation

**Customer Success Manager (CSM) — Post-Sale**
- **Target:** All DMU members post-signature
- **Responsibility:** Implementation, training, adoption, expansion/upsell
- **Messaging focus:** Fast onboarding, time-to-value, expansion opportunities
- **Success metric:** NRR; expansion revenue; customer satisfaction
