### Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) Calculation

#### Pricing & Revenue Assumptions

**Product Pricing (from company brief):**
- Annual subscription price: $1,200 per user per year
- Beachhead market target: 10-50 person agencies
- Average team size adopting ReportEase: 2-3 users per agency
- **Annual revenue per agency customer:** $1,200 × 2.5 users = **$3,000/year**

For conservative unit economics analysis, we'll calculate LTV per **user seat** (not per agency):

**LTV Calculation (Per User Seat):**

```
LTV = (Annual Price) × (Customer Lifetime in Years) × (Gross Margin %) × (1 - Annual Churn Rate)

Where:
- Annual Price = $1,200
- Customer Lifetime = 2 years (conservative; SMB SaaS typically 2-3 years)
- Gross Margin = 75% (typical for SaaS: COGS ≤ 25%)
- Annual Churn Rate = 10% (industry standard for SMB SaaS, not agency-specific)
```

**Step 1: Apply churn over customer lifetime**
```
Year 1 Revenue: $1,200 × (1 - 0%) = $1,200
Year 2 Revenue: $1,200 × (1 - 10%) = $1,200 × 0.9 = $1,080
Total Gross Profit (2 years): ($1,200 + $1,080) × 75% = $2,280 × 0.75 = $1,710
```

**Step 2: Account for CAC payback and operational costs**
```
Net LTV = Gross Profit - (CAC + Annual Operating Cost allocation)

Simplified calculation:
LTV = (Year 1 ACV + Year 2 ACV) × Gross Margin %
LTV = ($1,200 + $1,080) × 0.75
LTV = $2,280 × 0.75
LTV = $1,710
```

**Sensitivity Analysis: LTV Under Different Assumptions**

## SECTION 7: RISK ASSESSMENT & MITIGATION

### Critical Risks to Unit Economics

#### Risk 1: Trial-to-Paid Conversion Lower Than 15%

**Scenario:** Actual conversion is 10% (not 15%), lowering LTV:CAC from 5.7:1 to 4.1:1

**Probability:** Medium (20-30%) — current email data shows only 5/43 = 11.6% trial signup, not paid

**Impact:** High — reduces LTV:CAC below comfortable margin; limits acquisition budget

**Mitigation:**
- Implement onboarding analytics immediately to understand bottlenecks
- A/B test 3 different trial experiences (template-driven vs. blank slate vs. pre-populated data)
- Offer 14-day trials for engaged users (extend 7-day baseline)
- Create "winning first report" workflow (get user to completed report in <2 hours)

#### Risk 2: Churn Higher Than 10% (SMB Agencies Downgrade/Cancel)

**Scenario:** Agencies churn at 15% annually due to:
- Economic downturn (reduced agency budgets)
- Staff turnover (champion leaves)
- Product-market misfit (doesn't save enough time in practice)

**Impact:** High — reduces LTV from $1,944 to $1,665 (14% reduction), LTV:CAC from 5.7:1 to 4.9:1

**Mitigation:**
- Track actual churn monthly; set internal target of <8% annual churn
- Implement customer success program (monthly check-ins, usage alerts)
- Build advanced features targeting power users (retention levers)
- Measure actual time savings in product and share ROI reports with customers
- Create expansion revenue (additional seats, advanced features, white-label) to offset churn

#### Risk 3: Google Ads CPC Increases as Competition Grows

**Scenario:** Other reporting software (Whatagraph, Supermetrics) increases ad spending, driving up CPCs from $2.61 to $4-5

**Impact:** CAC would increase from $54 to $90-110, potentially breaking unit economics if conversion rates don't improve

**Mitigation:**
- Lock in current keywords with lowest CPCs immediately (increase budget rapidly while pricing favorable)
- Diversify into long-tail keywords and branded competitor keywords
- Build organic search and content marketing to reduce ad dependency
- Improve conversion rate (landing page optimization, retargeting) to reduce CPA even if CPC rises

#### Risk 4: Pricing Assumption Invalidated (Market Wants <$1,000/year)

**Scenario:** Pricing research shows willingness to pay is $800, not $1,200 (33% reduction)

**Impact:** LTV drops from $1,944 to $1,296 (33% reduction), LTV:CAC from 5.7:1 to 3.8:1 (borderline)

**Mitigation:**
- Launch pricing survey immediately (don't delay)
- If $800 is validated, shift to higher-seat targets (sell to 3-4 users per agency vs. 2.5)
- Consider usage-based pricing ($10/report or $500/month + overage) if headcount pricing weak
- Focus on expansion revenue (premium features, white-label) to increase LTV
