❌ "Enterprise-grade" (we're SMB-focused)

### Red Lines:
🚫 Never claim integrations we don't have (verify before messaging)  
🚫 Never say "8+ hours to under 2" without validation (test with pilot customers first)  
🚫 Never position as "free alternative" to anything (we're not free)  
🚫 Never claim 100% uptime, 100% accuracy (be realistic)

## Appendix: Talking Points by Objection

### Objection: "What if we go with Supermetrics? They have 100+ integrations."
**Response:** "True, Supermetrics has more integrations. But most agencies use 8-12 regularly. We prioritize those deeply rather than offering shallow connections to 100+. And at $1,200-1,800/year per user versus our $100/year, you can afford to hire someone to build a custom integration if you need something obscure. The ROI still favors us."

### Objection: "Whatagraph is cheaper than ReportEase."
**Response:** "Whatagraph is cheaper per month ($25-90 vs. our $100/year annualized), but here's the tradeoff: you're locked into their preset reports and 30 integrations. After three months, most agencies realize they need [HubSpot / Klaviyo / Amazon Ads], which Whatagraph doesn't support. Then you're paying for ReportEase *and* Whatagraph. We're cheaper overall."

### Objection: "We're already happy with our manual process."
**Response:** "That's great. Most agencies say that until they use a tool like this. The question isn't whether you're happy with manual reporting—it's whether your team could do higher-value work if they had 6 extra hours per week. That time is worth $15,000-30,000 annually. Wouldn't that be worth exploring?"

### Objection: "We'd need to switch all our workflows."
**Response:** "That's actually the opposite. We work *within* your existing workflows. You pull data from the same platforms you use today (GA, HubSpot, ads). We just automate the consolidation and delivery part, which is the tedious 80%. Most agencies are up and running in 30 minutes."
