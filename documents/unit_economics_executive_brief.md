| Path | Strategy | LTV:COCA | Status | Risk |

## PATH A: EMAIL-FIRST (RECOMMENDED)

### Overview
Pursue warm email outreach as primary customer acquisition channel, supported by content marketing. Minimize partnership spending in early stage.

### Unit Economics
```
Annual Pricing:             $1,200/user
Monthly ARPU:               $100
LTV (48-month lifetime):    $2,408
COCA (email-focused):       $1,100
LTV:COCA Ratio:             2.19:1 ✓

Key Metrics:
  CAC Payback:              14.7 months (EXCELLENT)
  Monthly Contribution:     $75/customer
  Payback Efficiency:       $1,100 recovered in 14.7 months
  Operating Margin Year 1:  Break-even
  Operating Margin Year 2:  38%+
```

### Go-To-Market Execution
**Months 1-3 (Foundation):**
- Build email campaign infrastructure (Warmbox, Apollo, etc.)
- Create 20-30 email sequences targeting specific pain points
- Develop warm list of 500-1,000 verified agency contacts
- Cost: $10K + 200 hours internal ops time
- Target: 5-10 trial sign-ups/month
- Target: 1-2 paying customers/month

**Months 4-6 (Validation):**
- Scale warm outreach to 2,000-5,000 verified contacts/month
- Test personalization and sequencing variations
- Refine conversion funnel (response → trial → paid)
- Cost: $15K/month (platform + ops labor)
- Target: 15-25 trial sign-ups/month
- Target: 3-5 paying customers/month

**Months 7-12 (Scaling):**
- Optimize top-performing email sequences
- Expand to referral program (warm introductions)
- Layer in content marketing (blog, webinars, case studies)
- Cost: $25K/month (email + content ops)
- Target: 40-60 trial sign-ups/month
- Target: 8-12 paying customers/month

### Financial Impact
```

Expected Customer Acquisition:       80-120 customers
COCA:                                $1,100 per customer
Revenue (Year 1):                    $96K-144K (from new customers)
Gross Profit:                        $72K-108K
Operating Loss:                      -$92K to -$128K (offset by co-founder investment)

Year 2 Impact:
  Email platform (optimized):        $96K/year
  Content team (scaled):             $120K/year
  Expected acquisition:              200-250 customers
  COCA (blended email+content):      $1,050
  Total customers:                   280-370
  Annual revenue:                    $336K-444K
  Gross profit:                      $252K-333K
  Operating margin:                  +25% to +40%
```

### Key Success Factors
1. **List Quality:** Target warm audiences (existing networks, communities, industry lists)
2. **Message Clarity:** Lead with specific pain point (6 hours/week saved) + ROI metric
3. **Follow-Up Sequences:** 5-7 step sequences (email + LinkedIn outreach)
4. **Product Quality:** Free trial must demonstrate value in first 2 weeks
5. **Customer Success:** Proactive onboarding to reduce churn (target <2.5%)

### Risk Mitigation
| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Email response rate <1.5% | Medium | COCA increases to $1,500+ | Use warm lists, personalize, test subject lines |
| Conversion rate <10% | High | Payback extends to 20+ months | Improve product UX, onboarding, demo |
| Churn >3% monthly | Medium | LTV drops below viability | Build customer success, add features |
| Longer sales cycle | Low | Affects payback, not unit economics | Free trial + product-led growth |

## PATH B: TIERED PRICING MODEL (PHASE 2)

### Overview
Introduce 3-tier pricing (Startup/Scale/Enterprise) in Months 7-12 to expand addressable market and increase ARPU.

### Pricing Tiers
```
Tier          | Price    | Max Users | Target Agency | Expected Mix
Startup       | $999/yr  | Up to 5   | 1-10 person   | 60% of customers
Scale         | $2,499/yr| Up to 20  | 10-30 person  | 30% of customers
Enterprise    | Custom   | Unlimited | 30+ person    | 10% of customers
```

### Unit Economics (Blended)
```
Blended Annual ARPU:        $1,849
Blended Monthly ARPU:       $154
LTV (48-month, 2.3% churn): $4,075 (with expansion)
COCA (refined):             $2,800 (less partnership-heavy)
LTV:COCA Ratio:             1.46:1 ✓ VIABLE

CAC Payback:                24.2 months ⚠️ (acceptable)
Expansion Revenue/Customer: $420+ (premium feature upsells)
```

### Migration Strategy
**Month 7:** Introduce tiered pricing
- Existing customers grandfathered at current pricing
- New customers offered tiered options
- Launch "Scale Tier" positioning (10-15 person sweet spot)

**Months 8-12:** Expand feature differentiation
- Startup: Basic reports, 1 data source, weekly delivery
- Scale: Advanced reports, 5+ sources, daily delivery, dashboards
- Enterprise: Custom integrations, white-label, dedicated support

**Year 2:** Upsell existing customers
- Target Startup tier customers for Scale tier upgrade
- Offer premium features (advanced dashboards, custom branding)
- Expected upgrade rate: 20-30% annually

## PATH C: PREMIUM POSITIONING (HIGH-TOUCH)

### Overview
Target top 20% of agencies ($5M+ revenue) with premium positioning, high-touch sales, and custom integrations.

### Unit Economics
```
Annual Pricing:             $2,500-3,000 per user (or $5K-10K flat)
Customer Profile:           CMO or VP of Operations at $5M+ agency
Sales Cycle:                3-4 months (consultative)
LTV (2.3% churn):           $5,844-7,025
COCA (selective):           $3,500-4,000
LTV:COCA Ratio:             1.67:1 ✓ VIABLE

CAC Payback:                22.4 months (acceptable)
Target Market Size:         5,000-10,000 agencies
Annual Revenue Opportunity: $12.5M-30M (at 100 customers)
```

### GTM Approach
- **Sales:** Inside sales team + executive outreach
- **Channels:** LinkedIn targeting, industry conferences, warm introductions
- **Partners:** Big 4 consulting firms, agency networks (SIMA, etc.)
- **Positioning:** "The reporting solution built for enterprise agencies"
- **ROI Messaging:** Calculate savings for specific use case (500+ hours saved, $200K+ in reporting labor saved)

### Financial Viability
**Pros:**
- Higher LTV than base case
- Pricing power (customers can afford premium positioning)
- Upsell potential (white-label, integrations, custom support)
- Lower customer count needed for profitability

**Cons:**
- Longer sales cycle (3-4 months vs. 1-2 months)
- Higher COCA (sales team salary allocation)
- Smaller addressable market (5K vs. 100K agencies)
- Higher implementation/onboarding costs

**Verdict:** **Viable but requires more resources and carries higher execution risk.** Not recommended as initial GTM strategy. Consider as expansion strategy in Year 2.

## CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

### Universal Across All Paths
1. **Retain Customers (Keep Churn <2.5%)**
   - Product quality is non-negotiable
   - Implement customer success program (email nurture, monthly check-ins)
   - Measure NRR (Net Revenue Retention) monthly
   - Target: NRR >100% (expansion revenue offsets churn)

2. **Deliver Measurable ROI**
   - Time savings: Quantify hours saved per week
   - Accuracy: Track reduction in manual data entry errors
   - Cost justification: Show payback within 6-12 months
   - Client-facing dashboards: Enable agency to show ROI to their clients

3. **Optimize Conversion Funnel**
   - Trial-to-paid conversion: Target >8% (from free trial)
   - Demo effectiveness: 40%+ of demos convert to trial
   - Onboarding: 90%+ of customers active within first 2 weeks
   - Feature adoption: Reduce time-to-first-report to <48 hours

4. **Minimize Gross Margin Degradation**
   - Infrastructure costs: Keep <$200/customer annually
   - Support costs: Target <20% of revenue (through self-serve, automation)
   - Integration costs: Build once, scale easily
   - Gross margin target: Maintain >75%

### Path A Specific (Email-First)
1. **List Quality:** Only use warm, verified lists (response rate >2%)
2. **Personalization:** Customize subject lines and opening for each prospect
3. **Follow-Up Cadence:** 5-7 touch sequence over 3-4 weeks
4. **Referral Engine:** Build network effects (free trial → referral bonus)

### Path B Specific (Tiered Pricing)
1. **Clear Value Tiers:** Communicate specific feature/limit differences
2. **Upgrade Incentives:** Reward long-term customers with discounts
3. **Feature Roadmap:** Commit to features at each tier level
4. **Churn by Tier:** Monitor if any tier has elevated churn (sign of poor fit)
